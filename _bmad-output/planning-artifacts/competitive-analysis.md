# Competitive Analysis

## Landscape Overview
Two distinct markets bear on WedLink's positioning: **global self-service wedding website builders** (mostly US/UK-centric, free, DIY) and **Greek full-service digital invitation providers** (paid, done-for-you, direct competitors). WedLink sits in the second category but should borrow specific mechanics from the first where they raise the bar.

## Greek Market — Direct Competitors

| Provider | Positioning | Pricing signal | Notable features | Gap vs. WedLink opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **mywedsite.gr** (primary competitor) | Full-service, 3 tiers (Kite/Balloon/Rocket), modern design | €150 / €260 / €370 | RSVP logic, seating + Pro seat-finder QR, hotel list, IBAN/IRIS, guest quiz, photo gallery, guest voting, live chat, 27 colors / 17 fonts | No real photo customization — color swatch only. This is WedLink's opening. |
| **i-do.gr** | Wedding + christening, eco-positioning, "video invitation" format | From €59 | RSVP auto-collect, quiz, guestbook, maps, budget/table tools | Lower price anchor — budget-conscious segment |
| **invitation.gr** | Digital + website + video invitation formats | Not listed | 4-section guest menu (RSVP, maps, gift list w/ IBAN copy, couple/sponsors info) | Simpler, narrower feature set than mywedsite.gr |
| **easywedding.gr** | Free wedding website + RSVP/budget/table tool | Free | Positioned as an Excel replacement for guest/table tracking | Free tier — competes on cost, not depth |
| **myikona.gr** | Print-first stationery company with a bundled free website | Bundled | Photo gallery upload, gift list, print+digital combo | Print-first brand; website is a value-add, not the core product |
| **weddinginvitation.gr** | Boutique/concept-driven, consultative (40-min call per couple) | 3 packages, not listed | Design-concept-matching service | High-touch, low-scale — not a volume competitor |
| **myweb.events** | Multi-event (wedding, christening, birthday, corporate) | Not listed | RSVP automation, QR sharing, broader event types than just weddings | Broader event-type coverage than most peers |

**Consistent pattern across every Greek competitor:** RSVP + maps + gift/IBAN + gallery + guestbook are table stakes. None offer AI-driven photo editing — customization everywhere tops out at a curated color/font picker.

## Global Market — Indirect Competitors (context, not direct threats in Greece)

<cite index="21-1,21-26">For most couples, Zola is rated the best overall choice for its unmatched registry integration and polished website builder</cite>, but <cite index="20-11">Zola's registry only ships to the United States</cite>, making it a poor fit outside the US. <cite index="12-4,12-5,12-6">Free builders like Joy and Zola already include RSVP collection and meal selections that some premium competitors charge for</cite>. <cite index="21-14,21-16">With Joy offers the most advanced free RSVP system, including multi-event tracking, meal preferences, and dietary restriction handling</cite>. On the invitation (not just website) side, <cite index="14-39,14-40,14-41">Zola doesn't actually offer digital invitations at all, Paperless Post has strong design but a coin-based pricing system that surprises at scale, and Withjoy is free but has known email deliverability issues</cite>.

**Takeaway for WedLink:** the global players validate that RSVP depth, guest messaging, and design variety are the features couples care about most — but none of them serve the Greek market's specific needs (IBAN/IRIS gifts, Greek-language RSVP conventions, local venue/map norms, done-for-you service model) — confirming the Greek full-service niche is the right one, not a general-purpose website builder.

## Positioning for WedLink
1. **Match** mywedsite.gr's full feature depth tier-for-tier (see `prd.md` Epics 5–6) — couples comparison-shopping must not see WedLink as "missing" anything they'd get elsewhere.
2. **Beat** every competitor, Greek and global, on the one axis nobody has touched: **AI-assisted photo styling** — a real point of difference, not a marketing color-palette-with-extra-steps.
3. **Keep** the done-for-you positioning Greek couples already expect (unlike the global self-service builders), but make the editor good enough that "done for you" can mean "done fast by the couple themselves," reducing the 5-day turnaround competitors advertise.
